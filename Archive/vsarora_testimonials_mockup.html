📋 Designer Notes — Testimonials Page
Clean, credible, and scannable. Every section here is about third-party trust — let the clients do the talking.
🔴 Hero + Rating Card
Dark navy hero with aggregate score card on the right (4.9/5, 80+ clients). This sets the tone before reading a single review — high score creates credibility bias that makes every card feel more trustworthy.
🟡 Filter Tabs
Service-based filters let a startup founder quickly find reviews from other founders — relevance dramatically increases conversion. Use JS to filter cards by data-category attribute. Default shows all.
🔵 Card Structure
Every card must have: star rating, service type badge, quote, avatar initial + full name + business name. Featured (navy) card = most powerful quote. Aim for 9+ cards across 3 rows minimum.
🟢 Google Reviews Strip
Two buttons: "View on Google" (links to Google Business profile) and "Leave a Review" (direct review link). Third-party reviews = uneditable = highest trust. Also improves local SEO ranking.
🔴 Logo Wall
Even text-based client names in a horizontal strip communicate social proof. Replace placeholder text with real client brand names + icons as soon as clients give permission. Keep it restrained — 6 to 8 max.
🟡 Testimonial Collection
VS Arora should actively collect reviews: after every successful registration, send a WhatsApp message asking for a Google review. A simple template message = 3x more reviews collected. Aim for 20+ before launch.
